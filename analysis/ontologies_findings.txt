Key Findings: Ontology Usage Across NIH IC Data Ecosystem Platforms
====================================================================

General
-------
- 25 of 29 platforms (86%) list at least one ontology.
- 4 platforms have no listed ontologies (NAHDAP, NICHD DASH, DataMed COVID-19,
  NEI Data Commons).
- 70 unique ontologies were identified across all platforms.
- Platforms use an average of 4.6 ontologies each (range: 1-23).
- 49 ontologies (70%) appear in only a single platform, reflecting high
  fragmentation across the NIH IC ecosystem.

Most Common Ontologies
-----------------------
- Human Phenotype Ontology (HPO) is the most widely adopted, used by 6 platforms.
- ChEBI, UBERON, and Gene Ontology (GO) are each used by 5 platforms.
- MeSH, OBI, Disease Ontology (DO), NCBI Taxonomy, and MONDO Disease Ontology
  are each used by 4 platforms.
- NIH Common Data Elements and NIF/NIFSTD are each used by 3 platforms.

Thematic Patterns
-----------------
- Phenotype and disease ontologies are well-represented: HPO (6), MONDO (4),
  Disease Ontology (4), SNOMED-CT (2).
- Anatomy ontologies are moderately adopted: UBERON (5), FMA (2).
- Chemistry and molecular biology ontologies appear across multiple platforms:
  ChEBI (5), Gene Ontology (5), NCBI Taxonomy (4), PubChem (2), UniProt (2).
- Clinical and terminology standards are less common: LOINC (2), OMOP (2),
  SNOMED-CT (2).

Fragmentation
-------------
- No single ontology is adopted by more than 21% of platforms (HPO: 6/29).
- The high number of platform-specific ontologies (49 appearing in only 1
  platform) suggests limited convergence on shared controlled vocabularies
  across the NIH IC ecosystem.
