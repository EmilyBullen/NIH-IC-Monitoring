Key Findings: Export Format Prevalence Across NIH IC Data Ecosystem Platforms
=============================================================================

General
-------
- 24 of 29 platforms (83%) list at least one export format.
- 5 platforms have no listed export formats (DataMed COVID-19, DataMed/BioCaddie,
  NIH Cloud Platform Interoperability Effort, NEI Data Commons, NLM Dataset Catalog).
- 70 unique export formats were identified across all platforms.
- 45 formats (64%) appear in only a single platform, reflecting high specialization.

Most Common Formats
-------------------
- CSV is the most prevalent format, supported by 17/24 platforms (71%).
- TSV (11), JSON (10), and TXT (8) round out the top four.
- These four general-purpose formats together represent broad, tool-agnostic data access.

Genomics/Bioinformatics Formats
--------------------------------
- BAM (7), VCF (6), and FASTQ (6) are the most common domain-specific formats,
  reflecting the prevalence of genomics-focused platforms in the NIH ecosystem.
- Additional bioinformatics formats with moderate adoption include BED (3),
  CRAM (3), FASTA (2), GFF3 (2), and BIOM (2).

Statistical/Analysis Formats
-----------------------------
- SAS format is supported by 5 platforms, reflecting NIH's clinical and
  epidemiological research focus.
- XPT (SAS transport format) appears in 2 platforms.
- Other statistical formats (SPSS, Stata, R) each appear in only 1 platform.

Document and Archive Formats
-----------------------------
- PDF (4), ZIP (4), XLSX (4), HTML (3), and DOC (3) indicate that many platforms
  also support human-readable and archival outputs alongside machine-readable formats.

Imaging Formats
---------------
- TIFF appears in 3 platforms; NIfTI (neuroimaging) in 2; DICOM in 1.
- Specialized imaging formats (OBJ, PLY, MP4, JPEG) each appear in only 1 platform.
